The primary focus of this visualization will be on life expectancy as a central measure of overall wellbeing around the world, and its relationship with other health and socio-economic indicators, ranging from GDP per capita to mortality rates, immunisation and education.

Life expectancy

Life expectancy has consistently increased over the years around the world, primarily as a result of significant advancements in healthcare, education, and various socioeconomic factors.

These improvements have contributed to longer and healthier lives for people around the world, reflecting strong progress in public health and wellbeing.

Further advancements in business, technology, healthcace and education will enhance the quality of life for people worldwide.

Life expectancy vs GDP per capita

In general, higher GDP per capita tends to be associated with longer life expectancies, indicating a positive correlation between wealth and wellbeing.

From 2000 to 2015, Rwanda saw remarkable gains in life expectancy, primarily driven by improved healthcare, disease control, and socioeconomic development.

Luxembourg exhibits notably high GDP per capita and life expectancy consistently over the same period.

Mortality related attributes

Global mortality trends have shown a significant decline, reflecting advancements in healthcare, improved living conditions, and public health initiatives.


Despite these positive changes, the African region consistently exhibits the highest mortality rates across adult mortality, infant deaths, and under-five deaths.


The European Union has consistently had a higher percentage of immunisation against the major infections diseases Diphtheria, Measles and Polio, compared to the rest of the world.

Immunisation against diseases

Over the years, the region has maintained a steady coverage percentage compared to other regions such as Oceania, North America and South America, where there are more minor fluctuations.

Schooling

Countries with a higher proportion of educated adults often have higher life expectancy.

Access to formal education contributes to better healthcare awareness, healthier lifestyles, and improved overall wellbeing.

The top regions in terms of both education and life expectancy are Asia, the European Union, and North America, showing the importance of education in enhancing public health outcomes.

Once again, Africa is the region which seems to be far behind the other regions, highlighting the need for targeted efforts to address these disparities.
