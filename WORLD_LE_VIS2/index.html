World Life Expectancy (2000-2015)

The primary focus of this visualisation will be on life expectancy as a central measure of overall wellbeing around the world, and how it is influenced by other health and socio-economic indicators, ranging from infant mortality rates and adult mortality to economic factors like GDP per capita.

3 mortality indicators by region

Global mortality trends have shown a significant decline, reflecting advancements in healthcare, improved living conditions, and public health initiatives. Despite these positive changes, the African region consistently exhibits the highest mortality rates acrossadult mortality, infant deaths and under fie deaths.

Bubble plot

In general, higher GDP per capita tends to be associated with longer life expectancies, indicating a positive correlation between wealth and wellbeing. However, there are exceptions, as the impact of GDP on life expectancy can be affected by other factors, including healthcare, infrastructure, education, and lifestyle choices.
